Approach vs. Answer — what's the difference?

Bad (giving the answer): "The fraud claim accrued on January 15, 2019, and the applicable limitations period under CPLR 213(8) is two years from discovery, so the motion should be denied."

Good (teaching the approach): "For each cause of action, identify (1) the claim type, (2) the controlling CPLR section, (3) the accrual trigger (breach, injury, or discovery), and (4) any tolling rules. Fraud uses CPLR 213(8)'s later-of rule. Breach of fiduciary duty is claim-form-dependent: three years for legal relief, six years for equitable. Cite New York state authority only; do not substitute FRCP or federal-court procedural rules. Use Bluebook short-form on second mention and include pincites on every cite."

The skill file should be useful for any similar task, not just the one you are testing.
