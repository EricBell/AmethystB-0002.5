Audit Findings Report

  1. Medical Decision Making (MDM) Element Analysis

  A. Number and Complexity of Problems Addressed: LOW
  The documentation reflects a routine check-in with a variety of patient
  concerns, none of which reflect moderate complexity or drive management
  at a higher level:
   * Anxiety / Panic Attacks: The patient presented for a check-in
     regarding a controlled substance renewal. He reported appropriate,
     as-needed use with no changes to the treatment plan. This qualifies
     as a stable chronic condition.
   * Lower Back Pain: The patient reported a recurrence of symptoms, but
     the clinical action was limited to discussing his ability to
     self-refer back to a previous specialist. No active treatment,
     medication adjustments, or diagnostics were initiated by the
     provider today. 
   * Morton's Neuroma / New Numbness: The condition is being monitored
     without active intervention ("continue wearing insoles"). 
   * GERD: Explicitly documented as stable with instructions to "continue
     current management."
   * Shoulder Pain: Documented as resolved requiring no active treatment.

  Analysis: Multiple low-level problems do not stack to create moderate
  complexity. Because the actively managed problems consist of stable
  chronic illnesses and issues deferred to self-referral without any
  changes in management, this element is scored as Low.

  B. Amount and/or Complexity of Data to be Reviewed and Analyzed:
  MINIMAL OR NONE
  The documentation does not contain evidence of any tests being ordered
  or reviewed, external notes being analyzed, or an independent historian
  being utilized. A patient's intent to self-refer to a specialist does
  not count toward data. Therefore, this element is scored as Minimal or
  None.

  C. Risk of Complications and/or Morbidity or Mortality: MODERATE
  The provider authorized the renewal of a prescription for Alprazolam
  (Xanax) 0.5 mg, a controlled substance, to be taken as needed. Under
  CMS guidelines, active prescription drug management inherently supports
  Moderate risk. 

  ---

  2. Overall MDM Determination
  Under the CMS E/M guidelines, the overall MDM level is determined by
  the highest level met by at least two of the three elements (the
  "2-out-of-3 rule").
   * Problems: Low
   * Data: Minimal / None
   * Risk: Moderate

  Because only the Risk element reaches Moderate, the highest level met
  by two elements is Low. (Note: Total encounter time was not explicitly
  documented in the note, meaning time cannot be used to override the MDM
  score). 

  ---

  3. CPT 99214 Compliance Ruling
  The billed CPT code 99214 requires a Moderate level of Medical Decision
  Making. Because the MDM scored as Low based strictly on the clinical
  documentation, the billed CPT 99214 is unsupported. 

  The correct and supported evaluation and management code for this
  encounter is CPT 99213 (Established Patient, Low MDM).

  ---

  4. HCPCS G2211 Evaluation
  HCPCS G2211 requires documentation showing that the visit's complexity
  is tied to being the ongoing focal point for all needed health care
  services, or for a single, serious, and complex condition. 

  While the provider clearly acts as the patient's primary care
  physician, the note itself reflects a routine medication check-in for
  stable conditions alongside a detailed narrative of the patient's
  lifestyle and history. A standard prescription renewal (even for a
  controlled substance) and the listing of multiple low-complexity
  diagnoses do not satisfy Medicare's strict requirement for G2211
  without clear, documented complexity driving the visit. Therefore, this
  add-on code is unsupported.

  ---

  Final Conclusion
   * Billed E/M code: 99214
   * Supported E/M code: 99213
   * Add-on code: Unsupported
   * Basis: The note reflects stable conditions and a prescription
     renewal. Only the Risk element reaches Moderate; Problems are Low
     and Data is Minimal, resulting in Low MDM overall. Time is not
     documented to override MDM. The documentation lacks the requisite
     complexity to support the G2211 add-on code.
