  Executive Summary
  A technical compliance audit was performed on the clinical encounter note dated 02/12/2026 and the

  Methodology
  This audit was conducted utilizing the 2021/2023 CMS Evaluation and Management (E/M) Documentation
  Guidelines. The Medical Decision Making (MDM) was scored across the three required elements to determine the
  appropriate E/M level.

  Clinical-to-Coding Correlation & MDM Analysis

   1. Number and Complexity of Problems Addressed: LOW
       * Clinical Rationale: The provider addressed one stable chronic illness (GERD) and one acute,
         uncomplicated illness/minor issue (new-onset peripheral numbness in the foot). The patient's shoulder
         pain was noted as "resolved" and the back pain discussion pertained to a planned self-referral to a
         prior specialist without active diagnostic workup or management by this provider today. This fails to
         meet the criteria for "Moderate" (which requires at least 1 chronic illness with exacerbation, or 2+
         stable chronic illnesses).
   2. Amount and/or Complexity of Data to be Reviewed and Analyzed: MINIMAL/NONE
       * Clinical Rationale: The documentation does not indicate that the provider ordered or reviewed any
         clinical laboratory tests, imaging, or external medical records. There was no use of an independent
         historian or independent interpretation of diagnostic tests.
   3. Risk of Complications and/or Morbidity or Mortality of Patient Management: MODERATE
       * Clinical Rationale: The provider issued a new prescription for a controlled substance (Alprazolam) for
         anxiety/panic attacks. "Prescription Drug Management" independently meets the CMS criteria for a
         Moderate level of risk.

  E/M Level Determination

   * Final MDM Level: LOW (Level 3)
   * Recommendation: DOWNCODE to CPT 99213.
   * Rationale: CMS guidelines require that 2 of the 3 MDM elements be met or exceeded to establish the overall
     level. Since we have Low (Problems), Minimal/None (Data), and Moderate (Risk), the highest level supported
     by at least two elements is Low. Therefore, the documentation supports CPT 99213, not the billed 99214.

  HCPCS G2211 Compliance Analysis

   * Conclusion: UNSUPPORTED. 
   * Rationale: The HCPCS add-on code G2211 requires the provider to document an "inherent complexity"
     establishing a longitudinal relationship for a complex or serious condition, or that the provider is the
     continuing focal point for all needed services. This encounter note reflects a standard, episodic check-in
     with a primary focus on a medication refill and minor, distinct complaints. The documentation fails to
     establish the specific longitudinal complexity required by Medicare to justify this add-on code.

  Final Actions
   * Challenge the $423.00 charge for the 99214 and seek an adjustment to the lower 99213 CPT rate.
   * Deny the $77.00 charge for the G2211 add-on entirely.
